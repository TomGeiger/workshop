Understanding Transfer Factors

Transfer Factors, often dubbed as "immune messengers," are bioactive molecules that play a pivotal role in immune system communication and coordination. Derived from colostrum, the first milk produced by mammals after giving birth, Transfer Factors contain valuable immune information acquired from the mother's immune system.
